Masters Project

My Masters work at UCL involves Matlab, Mathematica and Python simulations of mean-field and fully quantum descriptions of the Jaynes-Cummings system in the dispersive regime, with particular interest in the development of bistabilities in the system quasi-probability representations. Pictures at fergusbarratt.me are Matlab and Mathematica charts of: drive-detuning phase space of a driven Jaynes-Cummings system, and an n=5 Fock state Q function respectively. Bloch Sphere Visualisations with QuTIP

QuTIP is a FOSS python framework for modelling open quantum systems with particular applications in quantum optics. The two-level system (and analagously multilevel systems, though less usefully) can be simply represented on the surface of a sphere by way of a particular isomorphism. QuTIP provides a class for visualisation of such system dynamics.
